Our Mission

On our mission to become the first student team to achieve VTOL of a hybrid rocket, we can divide our objective into a series of well-defined short and long-term goals that guide our progress to make sure that our technical challenges are addressed systematically. These goals are as follows:

Short Term Goals (Achieved by the financial support of GTSF)

  • Milestone 1: Develop a Monoprop UAV as a test bed for avionics and algorithms (Aug–Dec 2024). GTSF will fund essential materials like the Teensy Flight Computer and counter-rotating propeller system.

    Proposed Project Dates: August - December 2024
    For this project, GTSF will be funding the necessary materials such as a Teensy Development Board (Flight Computer), CRM Motor Contra Rotating Coaxial double BL System (Counter-rotating propellers), DWM 1000 Transceiver (Radio Based Sensors), amongst many others that we will need to estimate our orientation in 3D space.
  • Milestone 2: Develop a functioning 2500 Newton-force hybrid rocket engine for ground testing and the hovering rocket.
    Proposed Project Dates: November 2024 - June 2025
    For this project, GTSF will be funding the primary propulsion device that goes on the hovering rocket. This is the engine that will power the flight and will be the one that we control precisely to achieve hovering, vertical take-off, and landing. The funding includes the fluid feeding system for our engine and the engine itself.

Long Term Goals

  • Develop thrust vector control with ±15° rotation.
  • Create a throttling system capable of reducing thrust to 40%.
  • Hover 5 meters with payload for 10 seconds.
  • Land safely from 15 meters with payload.
  • Ascend to 50 meters and land within a 10-meter diameter.
